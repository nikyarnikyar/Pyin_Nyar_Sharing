EQ in Leadership

Post by John Doe, Sep 24, 2015.
Lorem

The most effective leaders consider emotional intelligence a critical skill to recognize and solve their team members problems. That’s why emotional intelligence is an important part of many leadership styles. Emotional intelligence in leadership is referred to as the ability to understand and manage one’s emotions, as well as recognize and control others’ emotions and perspectives. The term is believed to be first used in 1990 by researchers John Mayer and Peter Salovey. However, it has gained popularity in the recent decade with the advent of leadership roles.
